# The landing is an engineering showcase on grid paper

_Accepted (design grilling + mockup artifact review, 2026-07-14; issue #316). Gives ADR-0047's landing exemption ("expressive landing, stock app — accepted on purpose") the spec it deferred: the landing stays custom, but custom now means this document, not the shipped page. The current composition — AuroraBackground hero with "Hope changes everything.", TrustStrip, FeatureBento, RearchitectureDelta, TechStackMarquee, FinalCTA — is superseded wholesale. Inspiration: voxyz.ai's content distribution and motion, explicitly not its handwritten typography or sticker kit._

**The story is the engineering, told plainly.** The page addresses reviewers and engineers, not donors: a production education platform, delivered to Fundación Vida Nueva in 2025, rearchitected in 2026 to run entirely in the browser. The mission-poster register dies with its headline — the owner's verdict on "Hope changes everything." is final. The provenance ("this shipped for real; what you're touring is the deliberate rearchitecture") is the page's strongest credibility fact and is layered rather than shouted: a two-date mono eyebrow above the headline ("Shipped to a real foundation · 2025 → rebuilt for the browser · 2026"), one clause of hero subcopy, and Q&A item 03 ("Is this a real product?") carrying the full account. The headline itself is a capability imperative in the "Run a school from your browser." direction — what the demo lets you do, with the exact EN/ES wording tuned at implementation.

**The landing owns entry, and entry is a persona badge.** With `/welcome` deleted (#362), the four roles enter here: ID-badge-styled cards — punch hole, initials avatar, seeded persona name, role, two lines of capability — flanking the headline 2+2 on desktop, collapsing to a 2×2 grid below it on mobile. Each badge is a real `<button>` that calls `setRole` and navigates via `landingPathForRole` (so the teacher badge inherits the golden-path drop onto its gradeable Course, ADR-0007). Teacher/student/TCU badges show the seeded people behind `tea-1`/`stu-1`/`tcu-1`; admin is a sentinel id, not a record, so its badge names the office rather than a person. No mascots, no illustrations — the badge metaphor is education-native and costs zero art. The fast path survives alongside: a fat "Enter as admin" primary button in the hero, and the nav's "Open app" pill doing the same.

**Skeleton: five acts, each with one job.** (1) Hero — grid-paper band, giant headline, persona badges, primary CTA + GitHub ghost, mono footnote ("no signup · data lives in localStorage · seeded with faker(42)"). (2) Proof marquee — a scrolling row of browser-framed app screenshots, pause on hover. (3) Numbered Q&A — "The questions you're probably about to ask.": 01 Where's the backend? 02 Is the data real? 03 Is this a real product? (full-width, with the before→after infrastructure delta table inline) 04 Why is everything bilingual? 05 Can I break it? (with stat NumberTicker chips). All answers inline — no accordion; the punchlines are the content. (4) Stack grid — a bordered cell grid of the actual dependencies with the message embedded in a center 2×2 cell: "No backend. It's all here." (5) Final CTA — badge row reprised small, primary button, footer. Plus a minimal real nav (brand lockup, language toggle, theme toggle, GitHub, "Open app" pill — no fictional Product/Pricing links). `TrustStrip`, `FeatureBento`, `RearchitectureDelta`, `TechStackMarquee`, and `AuroraBackground` die as components; every fact they carried is re-homed above, none is lost.

**The visual language is grid paper, one green, and weight.** The ground is a faint grid-paper texture (grid lines are the border token at low alpha) on the hero and marquee bands — blueprint/workbook, quiet, and what makes voxyz's density read as organized. The aurora is gone; it was this project's most-litigated slop. Display type is Inter Tight at 800/900 — two added weights of the face already shipped, no new family; the drama comes from scale (clamp to ~6.4rem), uppercase, and the single green accent word, not from a novelty font. Geist Mono carries eyebrows, numbering, and badge metadata. The accent is Figura green (hue 138) alone, used as: the headline accent word, highlighter-style smears behind two-three key words, and the primary CTA. No doodle arrows, no tape, no stickers. Both themes ship — every device above is token-defined, and the theme toggle stays.

**Motion is showy but purposeful, on the app's tokens.** The landing gets a bigger budget than the app but draws from the same `src/lib/motion` vocabulary: badges deal in with a rotation settle and straighten+lift on hover; highlighter smears draw on (scaleX from left) as they enter the viewport; Q&A items and delta rows cascade on scroll; the marquee pauses on hover; stat chips count up once visible. Everything gates on `prefers-reduced-motion`.

**The marquee consumes #314's screenshots — one pipeline.** The proof marquee's assets are the regenerated screenshots (#314), which deliberately wait for the finished post-phase-5 app look; the landing implementation is sequenced after phase 5 (#309/#310) for the same reason. Neither produces throwaway interim shots.

## Consequences

- `src/components/landing/` is rebuilt: `Hero` (badges, new copy), nav, proof marquee, Q&A section, stack grid, final CTA are new or rewritten; `TrustStrip`, `FeatureBento`, `RearchitectureDelta`, `TechStackMarquee`, `AuroraBackground` and the float/aurora-drift keyframes are deleted. `Marquee` and `NumberTicker` survive and are reused.
- Entry: badge clicks call `setRole` + `landingPathForRole`; the hard-coded `setRole('admin')` remains only on the explicit admin CTAs. Role-entry e2e moves from the deleted `/welcome` pattern to the badges (locators via `getByRole` with full aria names — #157/AnimatePresence scar tissue applies to the reveal animations).
- i18n: the `landing.*` namespace is largely rewritten in EN+ES (headline, subcopy, badge capability lines, five Q&A pairs, stack-grid center message, nav labels); `i18n:extract` prunes the orphaned keys. The illustration and its alt key die with the hero SVG.
- Fonts: `@fontsource/inter-tight` 800/900 imports added (~45KB); no new family.
- The landing exemption in ADR-0047 now points here; the certificate PDF exemption is untouched. No permission, seed, or STATE_KEY change anywhere in this ADR.
- Implementation issues are cut from this ADR only on explicit go, sequenced after phase 5; #314 regenerates screenshots first and the marquee consumes them.
