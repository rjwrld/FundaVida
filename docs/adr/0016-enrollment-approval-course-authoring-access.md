# Enrollment is an approval state machine; teachers author courses; students browse open courses by Sede and Level

Enrollment gains a status lifecycle — `pending → approved | rejected | withdrawn` — replacing the timestamp-only record, and two entry paths feed it: a Student self-enrolls into `pending`, while a Teacher or admin direct-enrolls straight to `approved`. Both paths hard-enforce two invariants and throw in the store before mutating (ADR-0009): the Sede match (ADR-0011) and a new Level rule — a Student may enroll only in Courses whose `level` matches their educational level or is `'both'`. Each Course carries a `capacity`; approval is blocked once the `approved` count reaches it, while `pending` requests may exceed the seat count, so a Teacher chooses among applicants. Teachers may now create Courses (matrix `courses.create`), but only at their own Sede and self-assigned as the Teacher, and each Course carries `status: 'draft' | 'published'` the Teacher controls — students never see drafts. Students gain a discovery read they never had: a new `'openForEnrollment'` scope token (ADR-0008) returning published, upcoming Courses at the Student's Sede matching their Level that they are not already in, backing a browse page and a read-only browseable Course detail (description, schedule, seats, a Request action) whose roster stays hidden. We rejected admin-published courses (a fourth approval gate against the empower-teachers goal), a soft or absent capacity cap (a weak invariant), and widening the `'enrolled'` scope to include browseable Courses (it leaks non-enrolled Courses into "my courses").

## Consequences

- The self-enroll path is upcoming-only (the `'openForEnrollment'` predicate); Teacher/admin direct-enroll may still add a Student to any non-completed Course (a late join). Both paths satisfy Sede + Level.
- Amends ADR-0011: Level joins Sede as a hard, store-enforced enrollment invariant — not a UI-only filter.
- Amends ADR-0012: a non-enrolled Student may now view a _browseable_ Course's detail with the roster still hidden — a third path beside the enrolled-self-view and the not-found denial for any other non-enrolled Course.
- Every new mutation (self-enroll, withdraw, approve/reject, create/publish Course) declares its cache-invalidation keys — rosters, "my courses", the browse list, and the dashboards that read them — and emits through `withAudit` (ADR-0005); `STATE_KEY` bumps for the new Enrollment and Course fields.
