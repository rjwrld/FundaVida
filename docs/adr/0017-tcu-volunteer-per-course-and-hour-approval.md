# A TCU volunteer is assigned to one Course, and that Course's Teacher approves their hours

The TCU model gains the two links it lacked. `TcuTrainee` gains a `university` (one of five Costa Rican universities, a Spanish proper noun) and a `courseId`: each volunteer is assigned to exactly one Course, a Course hosts at most three volunteers, and a volunteer shares the Course's Sede. `TcuActivity` gains an approval lifecycle — a logged activity is `pending` by default, and only the assigned Course's Teacher (or an admin) approves it, stamping `approvedBy` / `approvedAt`. Progress toward the 300-hour goal counts approved hours only: the dashboard sum that today totals every logged hour now filters to approved. This resolves the approver relation the old model left dangling (a trainee had only a `sede`, no tie to anyone who could vouch for the work). We rejected a join entity for the assignment (a single `courseId` expresses "one Course" exactly, and reassignment history is not demoed), auto-approved hours (it makes the approval gate and the Teacher's role invisible), and counting all logged hours (it lets a volunteer inflate progress with unreviewed work).

## Consequences

- The approver relation is derived through the assignment: a Teacher approves exactly the TCU activities of volunteers assigned to the Courses they teach — a new scope token over `tcuActivities` (ADR-0008) and an `approve` matrix cell (ADR-0007) for teacher (own-course) and admin.
- The TCU dashboard distinguishes approved from pending hours and counts only approved toward 300; the approval mutation declares its invalidation keys (the volunteer's activities, the Teacher's approval queue, the TCU dashboard) and emits through `withAudit` (ADR-0005).
- `TcuTrainee.university` / `courseId` and `TcuActivity.status` / `approvedBy` / `approvedAt` are persisted shape, so `isValidSnapshot` requires them and `STATE_KEY` bumps. University names are Spanish proper nouns, never passed through `t()`.
